# Parent Emotion Regulation App — Comprehensive Plan

> Status: **Concept / pre-validation.** This is a standalone new-product plan, unrelated to Get Lost. It shares this workspace only for convenience; it has its own queue, gates, and success criteria and must not inherit Get Lost's task IDs, beta posture, or release rules.
> Created: 2026-07-03.
> Working title: **"Steady"** (placeholder — naming is a Phase 0 task; check trademark before attachment).
> One-line pitch: *Not everyone can afford therapy or parent coaching — but every parent can afford 90 seconds of guidance, a breather, and proof they're not alone during a tough toddler moment.*

---

## 1. Problem Statement

Parents of toddlers (roughly ages 1–5) face frequent, intense, unpredictable emotional flashpoints — tantrums, hitting, refusals, public meltdowns, bedtime battles. In those moments, the limiting factor is usually **the parent's own nervous system**, not their knowledge of parenting techniques. A dysregulated adult cannot co-regulate a dysregulated child.

The current options fail most parents:

- **Therapy and parent coaching work but are inaccessible.** Individual therapy runs $100–250/session; parent-coaching programs like Good Inside cost ~$300/year. Cost, time, childcare logistics, and stigma exclude the majority of parents who need support.
- **Parenting content is abundant but wrong-shaped.** Instagram reels, books, and podcasts deliver advice when the parent is calm on the couch at 10pm — not at 5:45pm when a toddler is screaming on the kitchen floor. Knowledge doesn't transfer under stress; nobody opens a book mid-tantrum.
- **Meditation apps target the wrong moment.** Calm/Headspace assume you have 10 quiet minutes. A parent mid-meltdown has 60–90 seconds, one hand, and a screaming child in the room.
- **Parents are isolated and ashamed.** Losing your temper with a toddler produces intense guilt and the belief that "everyone else is handling this better." Shame prevents help-seeking, which deepens isolation. Existing communities (Facebook groups, Reddit) are unmoderated, judgmental, or dominated by advice-fights.

**The gap:** there is no product built for the *moment of dysregulation itself* — a fast, one-handed, judgment-free tool that helps the parent regulate first, then hands them the next right move, and afterward connects them to other parents in the same trench.

## 2. Hypotheses

Ordered by risk — each phase of the plan exists to test the riskiest one still standing.

- **H1 (core behavior — riskiest):** A meaningful share of parents will actually reach for their phone *during or immediately after* a hard moment if the app can deliver value in under 90 seconds. (Risk: in the real moment, the phone stays in the pocket. If H1 fails, the product pivots to the "immediately after / repair" moment, which is calmer and still underserved.)
- **H2 (regulation-first framing):** Parents will engage more with a product that says "regulate *yourself* first" than with one more child-behavior-advice product — because it names the real problem (their own overwhelm) without shame.
- **H3 (retention):** The natural frequency of toddler flashpoints (typically daily to weekly) creates organic recurring usage without dark-pattern engagement mechanics — if the in-the-moment tool works even once, the parent returns at the next flashpoint.
- **H4 (community):** A tightly-scoped, well-moderated peer space ("someone else's kid also bit a stranger today") reduces shame and materially lifts retention over the tool-only experience.
- **H5 (willingness to pay):** Parents who can't afford $300/year coaching will pay $50–80/year for an app that delivers a useful fraction of that value — the same "affordable slice of therapy" wedge that worked for Calm and Headspace against meditation courses.
- **H6 (side-hustle viability):** This can reach $2–5K MRR within 12–18 months on a content-plus-software product maintainable in nights-and-weekends founder time, with organic/social acquisition (parenting content is among the most shareable niches on short-form video).

## 3. Goal

**Product goal:** Become the thing a parent reaches for in the hardest 90 seconds of their day — a pocket co-regulator that calms the parent, scripts the next move, and dissolves the shame afterward.

**Business goal:** A profitable, low-maintenance subscription side business generating **$3K+ MRR within 18 months**, operable in **≤8 founder-hours/week** post-launch, with optional upside paths (B2B/employer benefit, pediatrician channel, or acquisition by a parenting/wellness platform).

**Explicit non-goals (guardrails):**

- **Not therapy, and never claims to be.** No diagnosis, no treatment claims, no "clinically proven" language without an actual trial. Persistent, visible signposting to real professional help and crisis lines.
- **Not a child-behavior tracker or surveillance tool.** The user is the parent; the product never profiles the child.
- **Not an engagement-maximizing feed.** Success is the parent putting the phone down calmer, faster — session length is a *cost* metric, not a win metric.
- **Not a Dr. Becky clone.** Her work is inspiration for the *category* (parent-first, "you're a good parent having a hard time," repair over perfection) — see §6 for the IP line we do not cross.

## 4. Target User

**Primary persona — "Depleted Dana":** Parent (skews mother, 27–40) of one or two kids with at least one aged 1–5. Household income too low or too stretched for therapy/coaching ($40–90K or high-cost-of-living squeeze). Follows parenting accounts on Instagram/TikTok, knows the gentle-parenting vocabulary, and feels *worse* because she can't execute it under stress. Peak pain: 4–7pm weekdays, bedtime, grocery stores, and the guilt spiral after yelling.

**Secondary personas:**
- **"Converted Chris":** The partner who gets the app recommended to him. Wants concrete scripts, not philosophy. Shortest possible path to "what do I literally say."
- **"Overwhelmed-by-content Olivia":** Has read all the books, follows all the accounts, is drowning in contradictory advice. Values one calm, consistent voice.

**Who this is not for (v1):** parents of children with diagnosed developmental/behavioral conditions needing specialized clinical protocols (signpost out respectfully); parents in crisis (signpost to crisis resources — this is a safety requirement, not a growth segment).

## 5. Product Concept

Three concentric loops, in strict priority order. The MVP is Loop 1 alone.

### Loop 1 — The SOS Moment (the wedge)

One giant button, reachable from lock screen / widget: **"It's happening."**

- **Step 1 — Regulate the parent (0–60s):** One guided breath cycle with haptics (works with phone face-down in pocket), a grounding phrase, and a reframe ("A tantrum is not an emergency. You don't have to fix it right now."). Audio-first, one-handed, works with the screen barely glanced at.
- **Step 2 — The next right move (60–90s):** Parent taps the situation (tantrum / hitting / refusal / public meltdown / sibling fight / bedtime). Gets ONE script and ONE thing not to do. Not an article — a card. E.g., *"Get low. Say less. 'I'm right here. I won't let you hit.' Don't explain the rule mid-storm — save teaching for later."*
- **Step 3 — Close the loop (optional, after):** "How did it go?" one-tap check-in → feeds the streak-free progress view ("14 hard moments this month; you used a breather in 9") and unlocks the matching **repair script** if the parent lost it ("What to say after you yell" — the single most shame-dissolving content unit in the entire product).

### Loop 2 — Between-Moment Guidance (retention/depth)

- Short (2–4 min) audio lessons on the *parent's* side of the equation: why your own childhood wiring fires during tantrums, the two-things-are-true stance, thresholds and triggers, repair as the actual win condition.
- Situation playbooks (bedtime, transitions, public places, hitting/biting, new sibling) — each a scripts-first, theory-second card stack.
- A weekly "one thing to try" — deliberately one, to counter-position against content overwhelm.

### Loop 3 — Community (differentiation, phase-gated)

- **v1 of community is not a forum.** It starts as anonymized, moderated *moment-sharing*: after an SOS, a parent can release the moment into a shared stream — "Toddler screamed for 40 min because the banana broke" — and others tap "been there." No comments, no advice, no replies in v1. This design delivers H4's shame-reduction with near-zero moderation surface.
- Full discussion (threads, cohorts, "3am club") only after moderation capacity, reporting tools, and community guidelines exist — see task queue. An unmoderated parenting forum is a reputational time bomb; we do not ship one by default.

## 6. Content & IP Strategy (the Dr. Becky question, handled explicitly)

Dr. Becky Kennedy / Good Inside is the inspiration for *positioning*: parent-first, sturdy-not-permissive, repair-centric, "good parent having a hard time." That positioning sits on top of a broad, public evidence base we can build from directly:

- **What we use freely:** the underlying peer-reviewed science — co-regulation research, Gottman's emotion-coaching framework, ACT/DBT-derived distress-tolerance and grounding skills, attachment and rupture-repair literature (Tronick), emotion-regulation research (Gross). These concepts are not owned by anyone.
- **What we never do:** reproduce, paraphrase-at-scale, or scrape Good Inside scripts, workshops, or podcast content; use her name/likeness in marketing ("Dr. Becky-style" in App Store copy invites both legal and platform trouble); copy trademarked phrases (e.g., "sturdy leader/leadership" as branding, "Good Inside").
- **What we build instead:** an original voice and script library, written in-house from the evidence base, reviewed by a **licensed child/family psychologist or LMFT paid as a content reviewer** (budgeted in Phase 1). This is both the legal firewall and a trust/marketing asset ("reviewed by a licensed family therapist").
- **Competitive honesty:** Good Inside has its own app at ~$300/yr. Our position is not "better than Dr. Becky" — it's *"the $60/year in-the-moment tool for the parents Good Inside prices out."* Different moment (mid-crisis vs. curriculum), different price tier, different depth.

## 7. Definition of Success

### For users
- **The moment gets shorter and kinder.** Self-reported: "I stayed calmer than I would have" on ≥60% of post-SOS check-ins by week 4 of use.
- **Shame goes down.** Baseline vs. day-30 shift on a 3-question parenting-shame/self-efficacy pulse (adapted from validated self-compassion items); target: measurable improvement in ≥50% of active users.
- **They'd be upset to lose it.** ≥40% "very disappointed" on the Sean Ellis PMF survey among users active 30+ days.
- **Safety line never crossed:** zero incidents of the app positioning itself between a family in crisis and professional help; crisis signposting surfaced on 100% of trigger-phrase detections.

### For the product
- **Activation:** ≥50% of new users trigger one full SOS flow (breather + script) within 7 days of install.
- **The magic-moment metric (North Star): weekly SOS users** — count of users who used an SOS flow during a real moment that week. Target 30% of MAU by month 6 post-launch.
- **Retention:** ≥35% day-30 retention, ≥20% day-90 (consumer-wellness benchmarks are ~10–15% day-30; the flashpoint frequency thesis H3 should beat them).
- **Session shape validates the thesis:** median SOS session under 3 minutes with completed flow — long sessions mean the tool is failing at its job.
- **Community health (when live):** ≥25% of weekly actives touch the "been there" stream; report rate <0.5% of shared moments; zero unresolved moderation flags older than 24h.

### For the side-hustle business
- **Model:** freemium. Free forever: 3 SOS flows/month + repair scripts (repair stays free — it's the trust engine and the viral screenshot). Premium: unlimited SOS, full playbook library, audio lessons, community. **$6.99/mo or $49.99/yr** (test $59.99/yr); founding-member lifetime tier at launch for early cash and testimonials.
- **Milestones:**
  - Month 3 (validation done): 500+ waitlist signups, 20+ problem interviews, landing-page email conversion ≥8%.
  - Month 6 (soft launch): 1,000 installs, 5% free→paid, first $300 MRR.
  - Month 12: **$1.5–2K MRR** (~350–450 subscribers), CAC ≈ $0 (organic short-form + SEO), churn <7%/mo.
  - Month 18: **$3–5K MRR**, founder time ≤8 hrs/week, at least one non-founder-labor acquisition channel working (SEO, App Store search, or referral loop).
- **Kill / pivot criteria (decide with evidence, not hope):** if after 3 months of live product the SOS flow has <20% weekly usage among actives (H1 dead) → pivot to the repair/after-moment product. If free→paid <2% at month 9 (H5 dead) → test B2B (employer parental-benefit, daycare/pediatric channels) before shutting down. If neither works by month 15, write the post-mortem and stop — sunk-cost discipline is part of the plan.
- **Unit economics sanity:** app-store fees 15% (small-business program), infra <$100/mo at this scale, psychologist content review ~$2–4K one-time + light retainer. Break-even at roughly 60–80 subscribers.

## 8. Competitive Landscape (abbreviated)

| Player | Price | Their moment | Our wedge against them |
|---|---|---|---|
| Good Inside (Dr. Becky) | ~$300/yr | Curriculum, workshops, community | 5–6x cheaper; built for mid-crisis, not couch time |
| Calm / Headspace | ~$70/yr | Quiet solo meditation | They need 10 min of silence; we need 90 loud seconds |
| Coa, Mindful Mamas, etc. | varies | Classes / general mindfulness | Not flashpoint-specific, not one-handed |
| Instagram/TikTok parenting content | free | Passive scroll | We're the *retrieval* layer: right script, right moment |
| BetterHelp / therapy | $260+/mo | Weekly sessions | We're the affordable slice, and we signpost up to them |

The category risk isn't a dominant incumbent — it's that "parenting app" is a graveyard of undifferentiated content libraries. The SOS wedge (Loop 1) is the differentiation; everything else exists to support it.

## 9. Technical Approach (sized for a side hustle)

- **Client:** Expo / React Native, one codebase, iOS first (target demo skews iOS; Android at month 6–9). Lock-screen widget + App Intents for the SOS button are the only "hard" native features in v1.
- **Backend:** Supabase (auth, Postgres, storage) — boring, cheap, already familiar from Get Lost work. No custom servers.
- **Content pipeline:** scripts and lessons as structured content (MDX/JSON in repo or Supabase table), shippable without app releases.
- **Audio:** founder-recorded or one hired VO session for the breather + top-20 scripts; ElevenLabs acceptable for drafts, human VO for shipped premium content.
- **Analytics:** privacy-first (PostHog or equivalent), pseudonymous IDs, no ad identifiers, no child data ever collected. Publish a plain-language privacy promise — the audience is privacy-sensitive and it's a marketing asset.
- **Moderation stack (pre-community gate):** report button, block, keyword/crisis-phrase detection with human review, published guidelines. Community does not ship until this exists.

## 10. Task Plan

IDs are namespaced `PREG-` to keep this fully separate from Get Lost's queue. Phases are gates: do not start a phase until the prior phase's exit criterion is met.

### Phase 0 — Validation (weeks 1–6). Exit: H1/H2/H5 evidence strong enough to justify building.

- **PREG-P0-001 — Problem interviews.** 20+ interviews with target parents (recruit via local parent groups, r/toddlers, Instagram DMs). Script focuses on the last 3 hard moments: what they did, what they reached for, what they'd have paid for. Deliverable: coded notes + top-5 flashpoint taxonomy.
- **PREG-P0-002 — Landing page + waitlist.** One page: the SOS concept, the price, an email capture, and a "which moment is hardest?" one-question survey. Target ≥8% visitor→email.
- **PREG-P0-003 — Smoke-test the moment (H1).** Ship a free, ugly web prototype of one breather + 5 scripts to waitlist parents; instrument whether anyone uses it *during* a real moment within 2 weeks. This is the cheapest possible H1 test and the single most important task in this plan.
- **PREG-P0-004 — Short-form content probe (H6 acquisition).** Post 10–15 short videos (faceless/voiceover acceptable) of individual scripts ("what to say when your toddler hits") with the waitlist link. Measures both acquisition viability and which scripts resonate.
- **PREG-P0-005 — Name, trademark screen, domain.** Kill "Steady" if conflicted.
- **PREG-P0-006 — Legal/safety baseline.** One consult: disclaimer language ("not therapy/medical advice"), crisis-signposting requirements, COPPA posture (no child users, no child data — document it), terms + privacy draft.
- **PREG-P0-007 — Psychologist reviewer sourced.** Identify and agree terms with a licensed child/family clinician for content review (retainer or per-piece).

### Phase 1 — MVP build (weeks 6–16). Exit: TestFlight build where a stranger can complete an SOS flow unaided, with clinician-reviewed content.

- **PREG-P1-010 — Content sprint 1:** breather flow script/audio, 20 situation scripts across the top-5 flashpoints from PREG-P0-001, 5 repair scripts. All original voice, all clinician-reviewed (PREG-P0-007).
- **PREG-P1-011 — SOS flow build:** big-button home, haptic breather, situation picker, script card, post-moment check-in. One-handed and audio-first are acceptance criteria, not polish.
- **PREG-P1-012 — Lock-screen widget / App Intent** for zero-friction SOS entry.
- **PREG-P1-013 — Auth + accounts (Supabase), free-tier limits, no paywall yet.** *Expanded and superseded by [ONBOARDING_REDESIGN_PLAN.md](ONBOARDING_REDESIGN_PLAN.md) (tasks PREG-ONB-001…008), which also covers first-run onboarding, permissions strategy, and the onboarding funnel events.*
- **PREG-P1-014 — Analytics + event contract:** define the ~12 events that answer §7's product metrics before writing instrumentation; pseudonymous only.
- **PREG-P1-015 — Progress view:** streak-free "hard moments handled" monthly view.
- **PREG-P1-016 — Crisis signposting:** static resources screen + trigger-phrase interception in any free-text field.
- **PREG-P1-017 — Usability test, n=8:** parents complete an SOS flow cold; fix top failures; repeat once.

### Phase 2 — Private beta (weeks 16–24). Exit: retention + PMF-survey evidence supporting a paid launch (or a pivot decision per §7 kill criteria).

- **PREG-P2-020 — Recruit 50–150 beta parents** from the waitlist; weekly usage review + 10 debrief calls.
- **PREG-P2-021 — Measure H1/H3 for real:** weekly-SOS-usage and day-30 retention against §7 targets; write an honest evidence memo.
- **PREG-P2-022 — Content sprint 2** driven by beta demand (which situations get tapped, which scripts get repeated).
- **PREG-P2-023 — "Been there" stream v1** (share-only, no comments) behind the moderation-baseline gate (report/block/guidelines shipped first).
- **PREG-P2-024 — Paywall + pricing test:** implement subscriptions (RevenueCat), founding-member annual offer to beta users; measure conversion intent honestly (beta users convert unusually well — discount the signal accordingly).

### Phase 3 — Launch & growth (months 6–12). Exit: $1.5K MRR or an explicit pivot per kill criteria.

- **PREG-P3-030 — App Store launch:** ASO around "toddler tantrum help," "parent anger," "gentle parenting scripts"; screenshots sell the 90-second moment, not the library.
- **PREG-P3-031 — Short-form engine:** 3–5 posts/week templatized from the script library (each script is a video); the repair scripts are the shareable flagship.
- **PREG-P3-032 — SEO layer:** 20 long-tail articles ("what to do when toddler hits you," "how to stop yelling at my toddler") each funneling to the matching in-app script.
- **PREG-P3-033 — Referral loop:** "send this script to your partner" — the Converted Chris channel; measure partner-install rate.
- **PREG-P3-034 — Android build** (gate: iOS retention holding at §7 targets).
- **PREG-P3-035 — Community v2 decision:** threads/cohorts only if "been there" metrics support it AND moderation hours fit the ≤8 hr/week founder budget; otherwise deliberately stay share-only.
- **PREG-P3-036 — B2B probe (optional upside):** 5 conversations with daycares/pediatric practices/HR benefits brokers about a sponsored-access tier.

## 11. Risks & Mitigations

- **H1 fails — nobody opens an app mid-tantrum.** *Highest risk.* Mitigation: PREG-P0-003 tests it for ~$0 before any build; widget/lock-screen entry minimizes friction; pre-planned pivot to the repair/aftermath moment.
- **Safety/reputation incident** (app perceived as substituting for needed clinical care, or an unmoderated community harm). Mitigation: clinician review, persistent signposting, crisis-phrase interception, community phase-gated behind moderation tooling, and the §3 non-goals treated as hard product law.
- **IP conflict with Good Inside.** Mitigation: §6 firewall — original content, clinician-reviewed, no name-borrowing, trademark screen on the brand.
- **Content treadmill exceeds side-hustle hours.** Mitigation: scripts are short and evergreen; weekly cadence capped at one "thing to try"; short-form marketing is derived from existing scripts, not net-new creative.
- **Category graveyard / undifferentiated.** Mitigation: everything ships behind the SOS wedge; if a feature doesn't serve the 90-second moment or its aftermath, it waits.
- **Founder attention split with Get Lost.** Real risk given the existing active beta. Mitigation: Phase 0 here is deliberately cheap (~5 hrs/week); an explicit decision point at Phase 1 entry on whether both projects can hold Stage-appropriate attention simultaneously.
